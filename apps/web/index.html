What you can do in the app

Everything below is designed around how people actually train—not around filling out endless forms.

☀️

Your Home snapshot

Open the app and instantly see how your week is going: sessions completed, training volume compared to last week, day streaks, and when you last trained. If you left a workout half-finished, you can jump back in and keep logging.

⚡

Log sets in a few taps

The workout screen is meant for sweaty hands and short breaks between sets—add exercises, record weight and reps, and move on without fighting the interface.

📆

History you can browse

Look back at past sessions whenever you want to remember what you lifted, spot patterns, or prove to yourself how far you’ve come.

📋

Saved routines

Build workout templates with the exercises in the order you like. Start a new session from a saved routine, or turn a great workout you just did into a routine for next week.

🔒

Optional sign-in & backup

Use the app fully on your own device with no login. When you’re ready, you can create an account and sync so your training history isn’t stuck on one phone—especially handy if you upgrade or switch devices.

🌍

Global & regional rankings

Opt in to anonymous benchmarks: we group you with people in the same benchmark sex category, similar age and body weight, and—when you both add them—similar height and years of training. Then we show where your last week lands—weekly load vs weight, session frequency, and cardio time from timed runs (e.g. treadmill). See “top X%” style insights globally and, with a country code, in your region. No public leaderboards with your name on them.

👥

Compare with friends—on your terms

Connect with people you actually lift with. You each choose what to share (like weekly volume, how often you trained, or a “best lift” label). Nothing is public unless you want it that way—friends only, with clear privacy controls.

💬

Training coach chat

Ask questions about programming, how hard to push a set, or recovery—like texting a knowledgeable training partner. When you’re signed in, the coach can use context from your recent sessions so answers feel relevant to what you’re actually doing.

Who GymBros is for

Same app—whether you train alone, follow a plan, or like a little friendly competition.

01

You just want a dependable log

You’re not chasing gadgets—you want to record sets, see what you did last time, and train even when the gym’s Wi-Fi is useless. No pressure to connect with anyone else.

02

You repeat the same days each week

You run a structured split and like knowing Monday is always “leg day” with the same flow. Save it once as a routine, start from there every time, and keep your history in one place—including after a new phone if you turn on backup.

03

You and your friends motivate each other

You want to see how your crew is doing without posting workouts to the whole internet. Share only the stats you’re comfortable with, with people you’ve accepted as friends—then keep the rest private.

04

You want context—not just numbers in a vacuum

You’re curious how your week stacks up for someone your size and age, or in your country. Turn on benchmarks when you’re ready; there’s no public board with your name—just anonymous cohorts and percentile-style readouts.

Privacy, in plain language

You stay in control of your training story.

🏠

Your phone first

Your workouts live on your device until you choose to sign in and back up. You can train for weeks without ever creating an account.

🤝

Friends only for friend comparisons

Friend stats aren’t a public feed. You connect with specific people, and you each decide which numbers they can see.

📊

Global stats are anonymous cohorts

Rankings use your synced training plus a benchmark group (sex category), age, body weight, optional height and training history, and optional country. You opt in explicitly. We compare you to other opted-in users in a similar bucket—percentiles and “top X%” style readouts, not named leaderboards.

🧠

Coach uses your context when you’re signed in

The in-app coach can read a summary of your recent training so answers match your situation—not random internet advice. That only applies when you’re logged in and using the feature.

Train your way

Whether you log alone or with friends, GymBros is built to stay out of your way—so you can focus on the next set, not the next menu.
